Destinations

Japan is a land of breathtaking contrasts, where ancient traditions blend seamlessly with cutting-edge modernity. From the towering skyscrapers of Tokyo to the tranquil temples of Kyoto, Japan offers an unparalleled travel experience for every kind of explorer. Whether youre seeking the hustle and bustle of urban life, the serenity of nature, or the mystique of ancient culture, Japan has it all.

Mount Fuji (Fuji-san)

Mount Fuji is Japans most iconic natural landmark, and it holds significant cultural and spiritual importance. As the highest peak in Japan (3,776 meters), it is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and those seeking spiritual renewal. Climbers tackle the mountain during the summer months, while in the winter, the surrounding area offers beautiful views and tranquil nature walks. The iconic symmetrical shape of Mount Fuji, often snow-capped in winter.

Hakone National Park

Located near Mount Fuji, Hakone is renowned for its hot springs (onsen), breathtaking views, and tranquil natural beauty. The area is part of the Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park and offers a variety of outdoor experiences, from scenic lake cruises on Lake Ashi to hiking in the surrounding mountains. Hakone is also famous for its historic hot spring resorts and its ability to offer a peaceful escape from the urban sprawl of Tokyo, which is just a short train ride away.

Nikko National Park

Situated in the mountains north of Tokyo, Nikko National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage site that combines stunning natural beauty with rich cultural history. The park is famous for its sacred Shinto and Buddhist shrines, but it is also home to spectacular waterfalls, lush forests, and hiking trails. Nikko dramatic landscapes are particularly striking in autumn when the foliage turns brilliant shades of red and orange, making it a favorite destination for lover photographers.

Budget & Plan for Mount Fuji (3 Days)

Budget: Allocate about ¥15,000–¥20,000 per day (approx. $100–$150). Accommodation: Stay in affordable guesthouses near Lake Kawaguchi or budget ryokan options. Transport: Use JR Pass or regional buses to reach the area. The Fujisan View Express is a great option for day trips. Eating: Try Hoto noodles (a local dish) and enjoy meals at budget-friendly eateries near the lake. Before You Go: Bring comfortable hiking gear if climbing, and check weather conditions (summer is best for summiting). Pack sunscreen and snacks for hiking. Budget & Plan for Hakone National Park (2 Days)

Budget: Expect to spend around ¥12,000–¥18,000 daily (approx. $85–$130). Accommodation: Look for affordable hotels or traditional ryokans with onsens for a traditional experience. Transport: Use the Hakone Free Pass for unlimited travel on buses, trains, and cable cars within the park area. Eating: Try katsu don (breaded pork rice bowl) or shabu-shabu at local eateries. Budget restaurants and convenience stores also offer affordable meals. Before You Go: Bring comfortable shoes for walking and exploring. Pack a light jacket, as weather can change quickly, especially near the ropeway and mountains.

Budget & Plan for Nikko National Park (2 Days)

Budget: Estimate around ¥10,000,¥15,000 per day (approx. $70–$100). Accommodation: Stay in affordable guesthouses or budget hotels in Nikko city center. Transport: Take a JR Pass for affordable train rides to Nikko or use the Nikko All Area Pass for local transportation. Eating: Opt for local favorites like yuba (tofu skin) or soba noodles at local restaurants. Convenience stores also provide quick, inexpensive meals. Before You Go: Bring comfortable walking shoes for temple visits and hikes. Check temple and shrine hours, as some may close early. Bring a power bank for phone usage in remote areas.
